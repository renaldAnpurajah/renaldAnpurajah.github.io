Sri Lankan's Google Search Pattern throughout the Economic Crisis - Animated Dashboard

A Data Story

Technologies used: Flourish BI and Excel

The Economic Crisis in Sri Lanka has had its phases. Covid-19 was an accelerating factor of the economic crisis. When we take a look at people’s Google Search Trends, we can clearly see how citizens’ priorities have changed over time. I have presented this using an animated dashboard in the form of modern Data Storytelling.

Has Sri Lanka forgotten Covid-19 due to the Economic Crisis?


Google search trends could say a lot about the masses' priorities. I have visualized how Sri Lankans' search patterns have changed since 2020 with regard to the following topics: Covid-19, Petrol, Fuel (in general), and Gas (LP gas is mostly known as 'gas' in SL).

Power cut-related searches have been trending ever since the scheduled power cut was introduced. The same goes for LP gas-related search since its shortage. Fuel-related searches have also been trending lately; however, they peaked in popularity when the National Fuel Pass was introduced.

However, while Covid-19 was constantly being searched since 2020, most stopped looking up information related to it as soon as the economic crisis led to shortages becoming apparent in early 2022. Note that a considerable amount of new Covid-19 cases and deaths were reported until the mid of March 2022.

It is almost as if the whole country forgot about the pandemic. Well, now that these shortages seem to die down a little bit and the Covid-19 cases are on the rise again, it might pick up traction again.
